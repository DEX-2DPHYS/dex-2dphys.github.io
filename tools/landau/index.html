Physical Inputs

B field (T) ?Magnetic field BQuantises Dirac electrons into Landau levels:E_n = sgn(n)v_F √(2eℏB|n|)Higher B → larger spacing. Drag on the fan map (bottom-right) to set B and n together.
Temperature T (K) ?TemperatureThermal Dingle factor:R_T = x / sinh(x)x = 2π²k_BT / (ℏω_c)Higher T suppresses SdH oscillations.
Lφ(1 K) (μm) ?Phase coherence length at 1 KSdH appear when Lφ ≥ C (orbit circumference). Model A:Lφ(T) = Lφ(1K) / √TLonger Lφ → sharper oscillations at lower B.
E_F (meV) ?Fermi energySets carrier density:n = k_F² / π, E_F = ℏk_F v_Fρ_xx peaks when E_F sits at the center of a broadened Landau level.
v_F (10⁶ m/s) ?Fermi velocityGraphene linear (massless Dirac) dispersion:E = ℏk v_F, v_F ≈ 1.0×10⁶ m/sAffects Landau spacing E_n ∝ v_F√(Bn). Substrate or strain can shift v_F by ±20%.
Path history pts ?Trail lengthNumber of stored points for the electron trajectory trail. Purely visual.

QHE Transport

Fan quantity ?Fan heat-map quantityρ_xx (log, longitudinal, including the n=0 neutrality ridge), σ_xx (log, conductance), or σ_xy (Hall conductance showing ±4e²/h plateau steps for graphene).
Dephasing model ?Dephasing modelModel A: Lφ drives orbit coherence; τ_q sets Dingle factor. Model B: fully T-dependent coupled τ_q(T) and τ_φ(T) rates for detailed WL/SdH fitting.
A_q (1/ps·K) ?Quantum scattering rate A_qModel B:1/τ_q(T) = 1/τ_q0 + A_q(T−1)Controls how fast oscillations wash out with T above 1 K.
A_φ (1/ps·K) ?Linear-T dephasing A_φModel B:1/τ_φ(T) = 1/τ_φ0 + A_φ(T−1) + B_φ(T²−1)Electron–electron Nyquist dephasing in 2D gives linear-T contribution.
B_φ (1/ps·K²) ?Quadratic-T dephasing B_φModel B quadratic term. Relevant for electron–phonon dominated dephasing at higher T in clean graphene.
Mobility μ (m²/Vs) ?Mobility μSets transport lifetime:τ_tr = μ|E_F| / (ev_F²)LLs resolved when ω_cτ_tr > 1. Higher μ → sharper QHE plateaux.
τ_q (fs) ?Quantum scattering lifetime τ_qDingle factor:R_D = exp(−π / (ω_c τ_q))Typically τ_q < τ_tr. Sets the B threshold above which SdH become visible.
α (meV/√T) ?B-dependent broadening αLandau level linewidth:Γ = α√BDisorder-induced broadening that grows with B in the quantum limit.
σ_xx peak (e²/h) ?σ_xx peak amplitudePeak conductance per LL in units of e²/h. Each LL in graphene carries 4e²/h (spin × valley). Reduce for disorder-broadened samples.
n* (10¹° cm⁻²) ?Charge puddle density n*Minimum effective carrier density from disorder. Below n* the system is inhomogeneous near the Dirac point and ρ_xy saturates. Typical on SiO₂: n* ~ 5–10 ×10¹° cm⁻².
