Model assumptions (read me)

This tool uses a semiclassical approximation. It does not solve the Dirac equation and does not include quantum interference, weak localization, universal conductance fluctuations, tunneling, edge-state quantization, or full self-consistent electrostatics.

The FEM mode represents local drift-diffusion transport. It is most meaningful when the mean free path is much shorter than the relevant device dimensions. The transverse Hall voltage emerges from the finite-volume boundary conditions and is qualitatively, not quantitatively, exact in v1.

The trajectory mode represents semiclassical ballistic or quasi-ballistic transport. It is most meaningful when phase coherence is not dominant and device dimensions exceed the Fermi wavelength.

The Landauer–Büttiker conductance in the sweep is the ballistic↔diffusive crossover: the Sharvin (ballistic) resistance from the trajectory transmission added in series with the diffusive (Drude/FEM) resistance, G = 1/(1/G_ball + 1/G_Drude). It reduces to the Sharvin ceiling when ℓ≫L and to the FEM/Drude value when ℓ≪L. A pure trajectory Monte-Carlo cannot reach the deep-diffusive limit (diffusion across the channel needs ~(L/ℓ)² steps), so that limit is supplied analytically via the FEM resistance.
